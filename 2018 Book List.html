Here is a list of the books I read in 2018. It was a great year of reading with a lot of great finds in non-fiction, fiction, and graphic novels.

Non-Fiction Books

  • The New Ocean: The Fate of Life in a Changing Sea
    Bryn Barnard

    A brief essay on the alarming fate of our ocean as pollution and acidity levels of the ocean rise, the temperature rises too as the diversity of life in our ocean's diminishes. This book touches on a number of important issues like the rise in jellyfish blooms; the ubiquitousness and dangers of too much blue algea as it turns our oceans to slime; dead patches in our oceans where life cannot exist; the introduction of carbon dioxide to our oceans which is causing acidification and melting coral reefs; the toxicity of fish like tuna which are high on the food chain and the effects that consuming these fish have on our health; and more.

  • Waking Up: A Guide to Spirituality Without Religion
    Sam Harris

    In the book, "Waking Up" Sam Harris tries to persuade his readers to reject dogmatic religiosity in favor for modern spirituality which utilizes scientifically proven techniques like mindfulness and meditation ranther than blind faith. This book was a challenging read, but intellectually stimulating and convincing in its central argument that science is not able to explain everything and so a kind of "religion" is warranted, but the precepts of many religions and cults, particularly the Abrahamic religions of Christianity, Judism, and Islam require us to follow strict dogmatic beliefs before any spirituality can be attained and thus are problematic. This book was a nice change in genre, as this year has been predominantly fiction.

  • Free Will
    Sam Harris

    In this light in weight, but heavy in thought book, Sam Harris makes the arguments that the notion of free will is an illusion. The fact that our brain fires neurons to react even before we consciously make a decision suggests that we lack free will. On top of this, the fact that thoughts, urges, feelings, and impulses do not originate in the consciousness, but rather appear in our consicousness begs us to question again whether free will exists. While I can't possibly pretend to understand all the deeper im;lications this book has to offer, nonetheless this book is a fascinating and thoughful read.

Fiction Books

  • Dragon Teeth
    Michael Crichton

    A historical fiction novel about the "Bone Wars" between then prominent paleontologists Cope and Marsh and the expedition that occured in the Summer of 1878. This story is told from the perspective of fictional character Johnsom as he joins the expedition upon a bet from his university rival. The historical perspective of Indian Wars then occuring in the west and the details of life in the wild west were very interesting and have throughly piqued my interest in reading further historical fiction. Highly recommended!

  • The Hate U Give
    Angie Thomas

    A political fiction young-adult novel about Starr, a sixteen-year-old who witnesses the brutal and unnerving killing of her friend Khalil when the two are pulled over and harassed by police. This book is timely as the "Black Lives Matter" movement is still being discussed throughout the nation. But even more interesting than the impactful storyline is the unique perspective of the author and voices whe gives to her characters throughout this beautifully written novel.

  • Aristotle and Dante Discover the Secrets of the universe
    Benjamin Alire Saenz

    A coming of age tale and teen romance about a teenage boy named Aristotle and hisdicovery and coming to terms with his sexuality and feelings for his best friend Dante. The inner turmoil and loneliness Aristotle struggled with struck a real chord with me as I reflect on my own experiences growing up, particularly his quietness as a means to protect himself from revealing too much is a problem I struggle with to this day. The drama that unfolds in this book are touching and the dialogue especially between Aristotle and his mother brought me to tears on many occasions. Highly recommended to any gay teens or anyone interested in a well-written coming-of-age story.

Graphic Novels / Comics

  • The Ghost of Gaudi
    El Torres

    A beautifully illustrated crime mystery surrounded by murders at Gaudi's revered architectural sites around Barcelona. The story is intriguing, well paced and thrilling. The character design and art direction are well realized and the attention to detail is staggering. The colors pop and the architectural illustrations make me want to visit these sites first hand. This book is brilliant in every way. I cannot recommend it enough!

  • Sheets
    Brenna Thummler

    A graphic novel about thriteen-year-old Marjorie who is in charge of her family's failing laundry business and Wendell the young ghost who befriends and ultimately helps save Marjorie's dwindling business. The story is perhaps too simplistic, but the artwork is beautifully drawn and the colors are reminiscent of an autumnal sunset. A quick, but fun read and perfect for Halloween.

  • Spiderman: Miles Morales, Volume one
    Brian Micheal Bendis

    This is a Marvel superhero story set in an alternate universe in which there are multiple Spidermen and Spiderwomen fighting alongside the Avengers. The story is fresh and seems relevant to our current society by managing to comment on issues like race, body image and gender equality. The style is bold and beautifully illustrated. I can't wait to read volume two!
